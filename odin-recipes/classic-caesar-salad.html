Classic Caesar Salad

Description

Caesar salad is a classic dish known for its crisp romaine lettuce and creamy, tangy dressing. The salad is traditionally topped with crunchy croutons and a generous sprinkle of Parmesan cheese, adding texture and flavor. The dressing, a key component of the dish, is typically made with ingredients like egg yolk, lemon juice, garlic, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and anchovies, creating a rich and savory profile. This combination of fresh greens and bold dressing makes Caesar salad a popular choice for both a starter and a main course.

Preparing a Caesar salad is straightforward, yet it allows for creativity and personal touches. The lettuce is usually torn into bite-sized pieces and tossed with the dressing until evenly coated. Homemade croutons, often seasoned with garlic and herbs, add a delightful crunch. The salad can be enhanced with additional toppings such as grilled chicken, shrimp, or bacon for added protein. Caesar salad is a versatile and satisfying dish that balances freshness and richness, making it a timeless favorite in many households.

Ingredients

  • 1 head romaine lettuce
  • 1 cup croutons
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ⅓ cup Caesar dressing

Steps

  1. Wash and chop the romaine lettuce.
  2. Toss the lettuce with Caesar dressing.
  3. Add croutons and Parmesan cheese on top.
  4. Serve immediately.
